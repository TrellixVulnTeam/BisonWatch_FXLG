# Beschreibung
Beschreibung :3 S: Typography is the art of manipulating the visual form of language to enrich and control its meaning. It’s an essential area of skill and, <br/>, knowledge for graphic designers. Typography predates modern graphic design by around 500 years; it is rich in rules, conventions,, <br/>, and esoteric terminology – but it remains an exciting space for invention and expression., <br/>, In this introductory course, we will study, name, and measure the characteristics of letterforms. We’ll consider the pragmatic concerns, <br/>, involved in selecting and combining type. We’ll peek into the rich historical, cultural, and aesthetic histories of familiar typefaces. We’ll, <br/>, discuss time-tested conventions and best practices in setting type, as governed by principles of hierarchy and spatial organization. And, <br/>, we’ll explore the expressive, meaning-making potential of type. Lectures will be complemented by a series of assignments. Please, <br/>, note that this is not a software course; a basic working knowledge of Adobe InDesign or other page layout software will be assumed. You, <br/>, will need access to a computer and page layout software, such as InDesign, to complete the assignments.
